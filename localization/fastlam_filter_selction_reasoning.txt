Why is EKF better for landmarks than particles?



- If we used particles for landmarks

Each landmark would need:

many samples
constant resampling
high noise sensitivity

Problem:

Landmark space = continuous + high dimensional
→ particles explode in cost
 EKF advantage for landmarks

EKF stores:

(mean μ, covariance Σ)

So instead of:

1000 samples

You only store:

2 vectors
Why EKF works well for landmarks?
-Landmarks are locally Gaussian

Once observed:

error becomes “bell-shaped”

perfect EKF assumption

Landmarks don’t move

-So their uncertainty:

only shrinks over time

-ideal for Kalman update

3. Fast convergence

Each observation:

reduces covariance

Particles alone would converge slowly.

 Why NOT particle filter for landmarks?

Because:

too expensive
redundant sampling
unstable map estimates
curse of dimensionality
